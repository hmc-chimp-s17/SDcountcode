================================================================
KEY INSIGHTS:
================================================================

1. Checking all three paths (v2→v3) has the largest impact, roughly 
   doubling or tripling the number of matchings with cycles.

2. The a-c edge splitting (v3→v4, v2→v5) creates additional cycles
   by inserting b as intermediate vertex, increasing results by 25-54%.

3. Different paths show different cycle prevalence:
   - 6J: s2-t3 > (s1-t1 = s3-t2)
   - PILLOW: s3-t2 > (s1-t1 = s2-t3)

4. Path overlap: ~16% of matchings (27/169 for 6J, 22/154 for PILLOW)
   have cycles in multiple paths simultaneously.

5. Symmetry breaking: For 6J, s1-t1 and s3-t2 produce identical counts,
   but s2-t3 differs. This suggests the middle path has special properties.
